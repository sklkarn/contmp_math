    Example 6.4.1.

    Classify the data into four types and give unit for numerical data.
    1. Salary (range)
    2. Car model
    3. Year of Graduation
    4. Years of experience
    5. Highest degree
    6. Computer model
    7. Number of children

    Example 6.4.2.

    For the following variables, give a name and indicate the type of variables (categorical, ordinal, numerical):
    1. Car owned by 10 friends
    2. Income of 20 employees
    3. Size of clothes as S, M, L, XL.
    4. Number of students absent for class.
    5. Education of people as undergraduate, graduate, Ph.D.
    Solution.
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|ccc} \circ & Description & \text{ Variable's Name} & \text{ Variable type} \\ \hline 1 & \text{ Car owned by 10 friends} & \text{ Make and Model} & Categorical \\ 2 & \text{ Income of 20 employees} & \text{ Salary} & Numerical \\ 3 & \text{ Size of clothes as S, M, L, XL.} & \text{ Cloth size} & Ordinal \\ 4 &\text{ Number of students absent for class } & \text{ Absentees} & Ratio \\ 5 & \text{ Education of people as undergraduate, graduate, Ph.D. } & \text{ Education level} & Categorical \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}

    Example 6.4.4.

    The mean of 20 observations are found to be 10 on rechecking, it was found that an observation 8 was incorrect. Calculate the correct mean:
    1. if wrong item is omitted.
    2. if wrong item is replaced by 12.
    Solution.
    using (6.2.5)
    1. \begin{align*} \text{ Incorrect } \bar{x} \amp = \frac{\text{ Incorrect} \sum {x}}{\text{ incorrect } N} \\ or, \quad 10 \amp = \frac{\text{ Incorrect} \sum {x}}{20} \\ \text{ Incorrect }\sum {x} \amp = 200 \end{align*}
      \begin{equation*} \therefore \text{ correct } \bar{x} = \frac{200-8}{19} =10.1 \end{equation*}
      \(N=19\text{,}\) since one observation is removed.
    2. \begin{equation*} \therefore \text{ correct } \bar{x} = \frac{200-8+12}{20} =204/20 = 10.2 \end{equation*}
      \(N=19\text{,}\) since one observation is removed.

    Example 6.4.5.

    If mean of \(y=5,\) then find the mean of \(x,\) if \(2x+2y =3.\)
    Solution.
    \begin{align*} 2x+2y \amp =3 \\ x \amp = (3-2y)/2 = -7/2 =-3.5 \end{align*}

    Example 6.4.6.

    Compute semi-inter quartile range and its coefficient from the following data.
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c} x & f_i \\ \hline 1 & 2 \\ 2 & 9 \\ 3 & 11 \\ 4 & 14 \\ 5 & 20 \\ 6 & 24 \\ 7 & 20 \\ 8 & 16 \\ 9 & 5 \\ 10 & 2 \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Solution.
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c} x & f_i & CF \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 9 & 11 \\ 3 & 11 & 22 \\ 4 & 14 & 36 \\ 5 & 20 & 56 \\ 6 & 24 & 80 \\ 7 & 20 & 100 \\ 8 & 16 & 116 \\ 9 & 5 & 121 \\ 10 & 2 & 123 \\ \hline sum & 123 & \end{array} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} Q_1 = \left( \frac{n+1}{4}\right)^{th} \text{ item} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} or, \quad Q_1 = \left( \frac{123+1}{4}\right)^{th} \text{ item} = 31^{st} \text{ item} = 4. \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} Q_3 = 3\left( \frac{n+1}{4}\right)^{th} \text{ item} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} or, \quad Q_3 = 3\left( 31\right)^{st} \text{ item} = 93^{rd} \text{ item} = 7. \end{equation*}
    Hence,
    \begin{equation*} QD = \frac{7-4}{2} =1.5. \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} and, \quad CQD = \frac{7-4}{7+4}\times 100 =27.27\%. \end{equation*}

    Example 6.4.7.

    Find the standard deviation, variance, and their coefficients of the data below: \(48, 43,65,57,31,60,37,48,59,78.\)
    Solution.
    Calculate mean of x using (6.2.1)
    \begin{equation*} \bar{x} = \frac{\sum x}{n} = \frac{48+43+\ldots}{10} = \frac{526}{10} =52.6. \end{equation*}
    Because \(\bar{x}\) has decimal point, it is easy to use assumed mean method. Hence, arrange the data in table as shown below:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c} x & d=x-A & d^2 \\ \hline 31 & -17 & 289 \\ 37 & -11 & 121 \\ 43 & -5 & 25 \\ 48 & 0 & 0 \\ 48 & 0 & 0 \\ 57 & 9 & 81 \\ 59 & 11 & 121 \\ 60 & 12 & 144 \\ 65 & 17 & 289 \\ 78 & 30 & 900 \\ \hline sum & 46 & 1970 \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Assumed \(A = 48\) and use (6.3.7)
    \begin{equation*} \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1970}{10}-\left(\frac{46}{10}\right)^2} =\sqrt{175.84} = 13.26. \end{equation*}
    From (6.3.8)
    \begin{equation*} CSD = \frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}} =13.26/52.6 =0.2520 \end{equation*}
    From (6.3.9)
    \begin{equation*} \sigma^2 =13.26^2 = 175.84. \end{equation*}
    using (6.3.10)
    \begin{equation*} CV =\frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}}\times 100 = 25.20/%. \end{equation*}

    Example 6.4.8.

    The annual salaries of a group of employees are given in tyhe following table. Find standard deviation and coefficient of variance.
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c} Salary (x) in K & No. of person (f) \\ \hline 45 & 3 \\ 50 & 5 \\ 55 & 8 \\ 60 & 7 \\ 65 & 9 \\ 70 & 7 \\ 75 & 4 \\ 80 & 7 \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Solution.
    For step deviation method,
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c} Salary (x) in K & No. of person (f) & d & d^2 & fd & fd^2 \\ \hline 45 & 3 & -3 & 9 & -9 & 27 \\ 50 & 5 & -2 & 4 & -10 & 20 \\ 55 & 8 & -1 & 1 & -8 & 8 \\ 60 & 7 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 65 & 9 & 1 & 1 & 9 & 9 \\ 70 & 7 & 2 & 4 & 14 & 28 \\ 75 & 4 & 3 & 9 & 12 & 36 \\ 80 & 7 & 4 & 16 & 28 & 112 \\ \hline sum & 50 & & & 36 & 240 \end{array} \end{equation*}
    here, \(h=5,\) and we set \(A=60\text{,}\) and using equation (6.2.4)
    \begin{equation*} d= \frac{x-A}{h} = \frac{x-60}{5} \end{equation*}
    using eqn. (6.3.12)
    \begin{equation*} \sigma = \left[\sqrt{\frac{240}{50} - \left(\frac{36}{50}\right)^2} \right]\times 5 =10.35. \end{equation*}
    from equation (6.2.3)
    \begin{equation*} \bar{x} = 60+\left( \frac{36}{50}\right)5 =63.6. \end{equation*}
    also,
    \begin{equation*} CSD =\frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}} = 10.35/63.6 = 0.1627. \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \sigma^2 =10.35^2 = 107.12. \end{equation*}
    using equation (6.3.10)
    \begin{equation*} CV =\frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}}\times 100 = 10.35/63.6 \times 100 = 16.27/%. \end{equation*}

    Example 6.4.9.

    Find standard deviation and coefficient of variance.
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c} x & f \\ \hline 0-10 & 15 \\ 10-20 & 15 \\ 20-30 & 23 \\ 30-40 & 22 \\ 40-50 & 25 \\ 50-60 & 10 \\ 60-70 & 5 \\ 70-80 & 10 \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Solution.
    For step deviation method,
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c|c} class & f & f_x (mid) & d & d^2 & fd & fd^2 \\ \hline 0-10 & 15 & 5 & -3 & 9 & -45 & 135\\ 10-20 & 15 & 15 & -2 & 4 & -30 & 60 \\ 20-30 & 23 & 25 & -1 & 1 & -23 & 23 \\ 30-40 & 22 & 35 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 40-50 & 25 & 45 & 1 & 1 & 25 & 25 \\ 50-60 & 10 & 55 & 2 & 4 & 20 & 40 \\ 60-70 & 5 & 65 & 3 & 9 & 15 & 45 \\ 70-80 & 10 & 75 & 4 & 16 & 40 & 160 \\ \hline sum & 125 & & & & 2 & 488 \end{array} \end{equation*}
    here, \(h=10,\) and we set \(A=35\text{,}\) and using equation (6.2.4)
    \begin{equation*} d= \frac{x-A}{h} = \frac{x-35}{10} \end{equation*}
    using eqn. (6.3.12)
    \begin{equation*} \sigma = \left[\sqrt{\frac{488}{125} - \left(\frac{2}{125}\right)^2} \right]\times 10 =19.76. \end{equation*}
    from equation (6.2.3)
    \begin{equation*} \bar{x} = 35+\left( \frac{2}{125}\right)10 = 35.16. \end{equation*}
    also,
    \begin{equation*} CSD =\frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}} = 19.76/35.16 = 0.5620 \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} \sigma^2 =19.76^2 = 390.45. \end{equation*}
    using equation (6.3.10)
    \begin{equation*} CV =\frac{\sigma}{\bar{x}}\times 100 = 56.20/%. \end{equation*}
